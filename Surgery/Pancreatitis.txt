Question 17:
Which of the following is NOT a typical etiology of chronic pancreatitis?
A) Gallstones
B) Chronic alcohol abuse
C) Cystic fibrosis
D) Hereditary pancreatitis
E) Autoimmune pancreatitis
Correct Answer: A) Gallstones
Explanation: Gallstones are the leading cause of acute pancreatitis but are rarely a primary cause 
of chronic pancreatitis, which is most commonly caused by alcohol abuse.

Question 60:
A patient presents with acute abdominal pain. Serum amylase is elevated. Which 
of the following conditions is associated with an elevated amylase but typically 
normal serum lipase?
A) Acute Pancreatitis
B) Parotitis (Mumps)
C) Intestinal ischemia
D) Perforated peptic ulcer
E) Pancreatic pseudocyst
Correct Answer: B) Parotitis (Mumps)
Explanation: Amylase is produced by both the pancreas and salivary glands. Parotitis causes 
isolated hyperamylasemia (salivary origin). Lipase is specific to the pancreas and remains normal 
in salivary gland pathology.

Question 15:
Which of the following is NOT a recognized complication of acute pancreatitis?
A) Hypercalcemia
B) Pseudocyst formation
C) Acute Respiratory Distress Syndrome (ARDS)
D) Hypocalcemia
E) Renal failure
Correct Answer: A) Hypercalcemia
Explanation: Hypocalcemia is a complication of pancreatitis (due to fat saponification). 
Hypercalcemia is a potential cause of pancreatitis, not a result.

Question 10:
All of the following are recognized causes of acute pancreatitis EXCEPT:
A) Chronic alcohol consumption
B) Hypocalcemia
C) Cholelithiasis (gallstones)
D) Thiazide diuretics
E) Mumps virus infection
Correct Answer: B) Hypocalcemia
Explanation: Hypercalcemia, not hypocalcemia, is a metabolic cause of acute pancreatitis. 
Hypocalcemia is a potential complication of severe acute pancreatitis due to saponification of fats 
in the peritoneum.
